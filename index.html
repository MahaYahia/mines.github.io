MINESWEEPERS ROBOT
"COMPETITON PROTOTYPE"

Our robot, designed for the Minesweepers competition, is built with an aluminum beam structure for the chassis, providing strength while maintaining lightness. The chassis is covered by 3 mm thick plywood for additional protection. The robot is wheeled, powered by four high-torque DC motors, which ensure smooth navigation across challenging terrains.
At the back of the robot, an arm is equipped with a wheel that has an encoder mounted on it for precise displacement calculation. There is also a dedicated compartment at the back of the robot for metal collection.
For detection, the robot uses induction coils to identify buried metallic objects, while an IR sensor calculates the depth of the buried metal. The robotâ€™s collection arm is fitted with an electromagnet that grasps the detected mines and safely deposits them into the designated collection box inside the robot.
This autonomous system was designed to meet the Minesweepers competition's stringent requirements, focusing on accurate mine detection, precise navigation, and safe handling of surface and buried mines, all with minimal human intervention.
